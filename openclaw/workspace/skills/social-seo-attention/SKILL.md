# Social Media, SEO, and Attention

Use for campaigns, posts, captions, keywords, titles, descriptions, hooks, content calendars, publishing, and performance analysis.

## Strategy before content

Define:

- audience and exact problem/desire;
- platform and content format;
- business or communication objective;
- primary action: watch, read, save, share, comment, click, subscribe, or convert;
- brand voice and prohibited claims;
- search intent and target query cluster;
- evidence, offer, or unique value;
- success metric and evaluation window.

Do not generate generic content before resolving audience, intent, and format.

## Keyword system

Build a keyword map from:

1. Primary intent: the exact phrase a qualified viewer would search.
2. Semantic variants: synonyms, questions, pain points, outcomes, entities, locations, and formats.
3. Long-tail queries: specific problem + audience + context + desired result.
4. Platform language: words used in comments, autocomplete, help forums, reviews, and customer conversations.
5. Exclusions: irrelevant meanings and low-intent traffic.

Assign each page or content asset one primary intent. Avoid cannibalizing the same query across multiple pages without a deliberate hub/cluster design.

Place important language naturally in:

- page title and main heading;
- first paragraph or spoken opening;
- descriptive URL where applicable;
- image alt text when it describes the image;
- video title and description;
- captions, on-screen text, and chapter labels;
- internal link anchor text.

Never keyword-stuff, hide text, create doorway pages, or generate mass low-value variations.

## Attention architecture

### Hook: first 1–3 seconds or first line

Use one truthful mechanism:

- precise pain recognition;
- unexpected but supportable contrast;
- result-first promise with a clear qualifier;
- curiosity gap that the content actually resolves;
- direct audience callout;
- credible data point;
- high-stakes mistake or misconception;
- demonstration before explanation;
- time, cost, risk, or effort reduction.

A hook must be specific enough to attract the intended audience and accurate enough that the content fulfills it. Reject deceptive clickbait.

### Retention body

- Establish relevance immediately.
- Show the outcome or roadmap early.
- Use short information units and visual/structural changes.
- Alternate claim, evidence, example, and implication.
- Remove repeated setup and filler.
- Place the strongest proof before attention decays.
- Use open loops sparingly and close every loop.
- Add pattern changes only when they support comprehension.
- End sections with forward motion.

### Call to action

Use one primary CTA matched to the content stage. Make it low-friction and specific. Do not stack unrelated actions.

## Platform adaptation

Do not copy the same asset unchanged across platforms.

- Instagram: strong visual, concise first line, readable caption blocks, accessible alt text, save/share value, public HTTPS media for API publishing.
- YouTube: title/thumbnail promise alignment, immediate delivery, clear structure, accurate description, chapters when useful, and retention-aware editing.
- TikTok/Shorts/Reels: visual proof in the opening, minimal preamble, vertical safe zones, readable captions, rapid but comprehensible pacing.
- LinkedIn: professional insight, first-line tension, concrete experience/data, useful takeaway, restrained hashtags.
- X/Twitter: one central idea, compact wording, strong first post, thread only when each post adds value.
- Facebook: community relevance, conversational framing, accessible media, question or discussion prompt when genuine.

Respect platform policies, disclosure rules, copyright, sponsorship, and sensitive-category restrictions.

## Search-quality rules

Follow people-first SEO:

- answer the user’s real query;
- demonstrate first-hand experience or reliable expertise where relevant;
- cite authoritative sources for factual or high-stakes claims;
- make pages crawlable and internally connected;
- use valid structured data only when the visible content supports it;
- preserve performance, mobile usability, accessibility, and stable rendering;
- avoid spam tactics and content created only to manipulate rankings.

Search visibility is not guaranteed. Never promise a ranking.

## Content production checklist

Before approval:

- claim is accurate and supportable;
- hook and body deliver the same promise;
- first frame/line is understandable without context;
- brand voice is consistent;
- spelling, captions, links, and CTA are correct;
- media aspect ratio and safe areas match the platform;
- accessibility text is meaningful;
- licensed assets and disclosures are present;
- no private data or unapproved testimonial appears;
- platform account and target are correct.

## Publishing workflow

For automated publishing:

1. Verify connected account and permissions.
2. Validate platform/content-type mapping.
3. Persist generated media to a durable public HTTPS URL.
4. Confirm Meta or other platform crawlers can fetch it without authentication.
5. Use an idempotent scheduled-post record.
6. Require a provider container/post/media ID.
7. Store result, error, and publication timestamp.
8. Reconcile timeouts before retrying.
9. Reschedule recurring content only after verified success.

A generated `data:` URL or loopback URL is not publishable media for Instagram.

## Measurement and iteration

Track metrics appropriate to the goal:

- impressions and qualified reach;
- click-through rate;
- watch time and retention curve;
- saves, shares, comments, and follows;
- search impressions, clicks, position, and conversions;
- landing-page engagement and downstream outcome.

Compare like with like. Change one major variable per test: hook, title, thumbnail, format, CTA, audience, or posting time. Use a sufficient sample and document confounders.

## Anti-hallucination rules

- Do not invent trending keywords, platform algorithm changes, search volume, engagement benchmarks, or performance results.
- Use current platform or analytics data when available.
- Label creative hypotheses as hypotheses.
- Never report a post published without a real platform ID or verified result.

## Primary guidance

Follow current Google Search Essentials for technical eligibility, spam policies, and people-first content. For YouTube discovery, optimize for relevance, engagement, and quality without claiming secret ranking factors. Use current official platform API and policy documentation before automating publication.
